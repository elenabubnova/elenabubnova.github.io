BMC Self-Guided Demo
Improving web experience

My Role: UX / Visual Design / Web

Background

The self-guided demo lets users explore products in a real environment. They can follow scenarios, use preset data, or explore freely.

The gap between form completions and effective registrations

Analytics and UX research revealed a disconnect between completed forms and actual sign-ups. Many users filled out forms but never completed registration. We needed to identify and fix the bottleneck.

Customer Journey

The journey spans multiple touchpoints: finding the demo, completing a form, email verification, SSO registration, product portal access, and the demo itself. Each step is critical to user activation and sales conversion. Any friction in this flow led to drop-offs.

My Role

I worked with a Researcher, Content Strategist, and Product Manager. I led the trial experience design, including onboarding, activation, and Helix Portal welcome screens, KC bot and nurture emails.

User Research & Methods

Along with researcher I conducted competitive analysis, heuristic evaluation, and usability testing. Key findings showed unclear messaging, poor activation emails, confusing registration, and brand inconsistencies. These issues caused hesitation, drop-offs, and unmet expectations.

    Key findings:

  1. Lack of clarity.

    On the website trial page. Lack of information. Many pogo-stick behavioral, complicated registration form.

  2. Unrecognizable activation email.

    With no mention of “trial” and no description of the Helix Portal.

  3. Confusing setup and experience.

    Ability to setup trials for multiple products at one time.

  4. Misleading language.

    Wrong expectations what is Demo.

  5. No easy access to get help.

    No contact inside the demo.

  6. Brand inconsistency.

    Multiple brand styles.

Usability Testing

I formed hypotheses on filtering, previews, and next-step uncertainty. I designed a prototype and ran usability testing with potential users. The feedback was essential in refining the experience.

Feedback

Users completed tasks but found "Guided Demo" unclear and expected follow-ups after form submission. They struggled to find the right product and didn’t use filters. These insights shaped our design improvements.

Improvements on Trial page

I redesigned the trial page for better usability and clarity. Key changes included dual-button cards for detailed product info, tab-based navigation, and clearer product descriptions. We also improved the Helix Portal onboarding experience.

Registration Fixes

We clarified expectations on the form and thank-you pages. Video snippets gave users a preview of the experience before entering the demo. The term "Guided Demo" changed to "Self-Guided Tour" to remove confusion.

Improvements Inside the Demo

We simplified backend registration and redesigned the welcome screen with clearer use cases. New intro messages, a contact CTA, and a character improved consistency. A contact form at the end ensured users could get help.

Email Reminders

Users were overwhelmed by too many emails, causing important ones to be ignored. We redesigned activation and nurture emails with clearer subject lines, credential reminders, and consistent branding. This ensured users followed through with their demo.

Improved onboarding

Users struggled with mixed guided content and UI elements. We modified the KC bot to prevent confusion with a real agent chat. We added video-based guidance, step-by-step overlays, and tooltips. These changes made onboarding smoother and reduced user friction.

Reflection & Takeaways

This project reinforced the importance of research, iteration, and collaboration. Rapid prototyping and testing helped refine the experience. Working with stakeholders ensured a balance between technical constraints and design impact.
