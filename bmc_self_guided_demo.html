The gap between form completions and effective registrations

The self-guided demo allows users to quickly explore products and features in a real environment by giving scenarios, with preset data, or by exploring at their own pace. The user journey includes many touchpoints, including the website, email, product portal, and demo itself.

Due to outsourcing vendor-limitations projects required many usability improvements. Along with other issues analytics and UX research revealed the gap between completed forms on the website and actual sign-ups.

My role

I led the website part of the user journey including the initial onboarding experience, activation, and nurture email. I collaborated with two other designers on the Helix portal home screen and KC bot.

In addition, I worked alongside a Researcher, Content Strategist, and Product Manager.

Research methods

I made my internal research and iterations to find drawbacks and out-of-scope opportunities for improvements.

  • Competitive Analysis
  • Heuristic evaluation
  • Usability testing

Feedback

Overall users were able to complete tasks successfully without confusion. The flow of the product page was particularly natural and intuitive. However, the term "Guided Demo" was not clear to users and caused some confusion. Users reported difficulty finding the right product. Many users expressed an expectation that they would be contacted after submitting a form.

Improved onboarding

After usability testing, we found room for improvement in clarity and ease of digesting information, especially when guided content was mixed with the application interface.

  • Added video format for use cases to modal windows
  • Revised Step-by-step overlays introducing users to key features.
  • Added tooltips
  • Improved UI for better stand-out
