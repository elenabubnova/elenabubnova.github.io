BMC.com Product page redesign Improving application experience

My Role: UX / Prototyping / Design Direction

Goal

Accelerate BMC's transformation and reputation as a premier provider of SaaS solutions by producing web experiences that are compelling, customer-centric, easy to understand, leverage innovative design patterns and interactivity, and support a long-term customer relationship.

Stretch goals:

  1. Non-paid form page conversion rate: 35% (75% increase from 2022)
  2. Paid form page conversion rate: 9% (50% increases from 2022)

Research

Research methods:

  1. Web analytics (Data, Heatmaps, Recordings)
  2. Secondary research
  3. Interview with customer
  4. Competitive Analysis
  5. Usability testing

The most valuable information we received during the interview session. The “Practitioner” persona really didn't use the website as a source of truth and went straight to the Support or Documentation section. The “Evaluator” persona prefers to trust other resources such as Gartner or Forester. Both the language and perception of the page felt “too marketing” and far from their needs.

Competitive Analysis

We carefully reviewed product pages from different companies, including well-known brands and direct SaaS competitors. Our analysis included aspects such as messaging, the count of CTAs, the presence of human photographs, and more. Additionally, we studied organic search traffic, page authority score, and top keywords to compare the performance of these product pages on Google with our own. This research provided us with insights to establish a basis for structuring product page design and messaging.

Key findings

Research methods:

  1. Lack of personalisation In the structure and language
  2. Website doesn't represent SaaS Lack of self exploration
  3. Users prefer side materials Datasheets and Whitepapers
  4. Lack of alignment with user mental model Complicated product naming including navigation
  5. Complicated marketing language Lack of addressing user needs
  6. Brand perception Lack of trust to the brand

Persona

Based on the research we created 2 personas: Functional or Practitioner (Manager, Technical) who evaluate this product and make preparation for the decision maker — Strategic leader (IT Ops Directors, VPs), Second Persona.

We designed 2 different journeys and planned to trigger Cookie to identify Persona and personalize content relevant to their specific needs. During the discussion, there was a decision to keep the flow for both on the same page. This means there will be 2 types of content and messaging.

Wireframe

We went through the ideation process and many iterations and finally packed it into the wireframe. My main idea was to convert the language to actionable that resonated with user flow. Check, try, integrate, learn more, decide. I built sectioning based on that flow.

I also provided direction and handed it off to Creative Service who was responsible for the visual part of this project.
